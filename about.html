Did You Know?

Fun Facts About Pittsburgh

446
Bridges
Pittsburgh has more bridges than any other city in the world (yes, including Venice). Three rivers (the Allegheny, Monongahela, and Ohio) converge right here.
90+
Neighborhoods
Pittsburgh is made up of over 90 distinct neighborhoods, each with its own culture, history, and personality — from the Strip District to Lawrenceville to Squirrel Hill.
2+
World-Class Universities
Carnegie Mellon University and the University of Pittsburgh make Pittsburgh a global hub for technology, medicine, and entrepreneurship.

While You're Here

Must-See Pittsburgh

If you have free time before or after the retreat, here are some of our favorites.

The Andy Warhol Museum
Pittsburgh's most famous son gets his own museum — 7 floors of pop art and cultural history right on the North Shore.
The Duquesne Incline
A historic cable car (funicular) up Mount Washington with one of the most iconic views of the Pittsburgh skyline. Don't miss the Golden Triangle at sunset.
Phipps Conservatory
A stunning Victorian greenhouse with rotating botanical exhibitions, located right in Schenley Park near CMU.
The Strip District
Pittsburgh's bustling market district — fresh produce, international grocers, pierogies, coffee, and the beloved Primanti Brothers original location.
Carnegie Museum of Art
World-class collection steps from CMU — contemporary art, architecture, and design in a grand Beaux-Arts building.
Schenley Park
456 acres of urban park with trails, a skating rink, tennis courts, and gorgeous views. Perfect for a morning run between retreat sessions.
