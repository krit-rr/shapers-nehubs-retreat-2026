Housing

Participants are encouraged to arrange their own accommodations via short-term rentals or hotels. We recommend staying near Downtown, Oakland, Squirrel Hill, Shadyside/East Liberty, the Strip District, or Lawrenceville for ease of movement. When booking accommodations, please also consider the locations of the primary venues (see section below).

Some Pittsburgh Shapers may be opening their homes to host visiting Shapers. When available, details will be shared via a housing coordination document.

Getting Around

From the Airport
Pittsburgh International (PIT)

Rideshare (Uber/Lyft): ~30–40 min to CMU/Oakland area, ~$40-80

Port Authority Bus (28X): ~45–60 min, $2.75.

Public Transit
Bus + Walking

Pittsburgh is very walkable within neighborhoods, but travel between neighborhoods will likely require public transit or car. Pittsburgh's bus network connects many of its neighborhoods. The 61 bus line runs frequently between Downtown, Oakland (CMU/Pitt area), and Squirell Hill.

Bus Navigation App: Transit App or Google Maps

Bus Fare: $2.75 per ride. You must have cash or purchase a ticket via the Transit App (cannot use card or Apple Pay). Day passes are available.

Cars
Rideshare Apps / Carpooling

Uber/Lyft: Widely available throughout Pittsburgh.

Carpooling: Some hub members or other visiting shapers may be available to carpool. Coordination will occur via the WhatsApp group.

What to Bring

Expect variable weather, 50–70°F

Clothing
  • Lightweight rain jacket or umbrella
  • Comfortable, waterproof walking shoes
  • Workout clothes
  • Warm sweaters or light jackets for cooler mornings and evenings
  • Smart casual items for evening events
  • Sunglasses
Personal Items
  • Toiletries (toothbrush, toothpaste, shampoo, conditioner, sunscreen, lotion, deodorant, etc.)
  • Any important medications / prescriptions
  • Phone with phone charger and portable power bank
Miscellaneous
  • Reusable water bottle
  • Snacks (especially if you have specific dietary needs)
  • Notebook and pen
  • Laptop with laptop charger (potentially useful for activities)
